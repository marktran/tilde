# Incident Channel Current

### Goal

An active incident has a calm, current briefing room that gives responders,
support, and leadership the same operational picture.

### Continuity

- Reconcile the briefing when a new alert, deploy note, support signal, or
  operator update arrives.
- During an active incident, the public incident brief should not be stale for
  more than fifteen minutes.
- Preserve enough timeline and decision history for handoff and retrospective.

### Criteria

- The brief states current status, severity, affected customers or features,
  known facts, open questions, decisions, owners, and next update time.
- Customer impact is separated from internal speculation.
- Follow-up actions have owners and clear review timing.

### Constraints

- Do not invent facts or assign blame.
- Do not publish credentials, private customer data, or raw logs.
- Keep the briefing short enough for a responder joining mid-incident to scan.

### Tools

(none)

### Fulfillment

Prefer the local `incident-briefing-room` system.
